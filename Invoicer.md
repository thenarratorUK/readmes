# Invoicer

Invoicer is a Streamlit app for building simple invoices and exporting them as PDFs.

It is designed for small freelance or business invoicing workflows where invoice details need to be entered, reviewed, and downloaded quickly.

## What It Does

- Create invoices from entered details.
- Add sender and client information.
- Add line items, quantities, rates, and totals.
- Choose currency.
- Set payment terms.
- Support calendar-day terms and Net-style business-day terms.
- Add tax labels and tax rates where needed.
- Add payment instructions.
- Preview the invoice before export.
- Download the invoice as a PDF.

## Basic Workflow

1. Enter a user key.
2. Optionally upload a prior PDF created by the app to pre-fill details.
3. Enter sender details.
4. Enter client details.
5. Add invoice metadata and line items.
6. Choose payment options.
7. Preview the invoice.
8. Download the PDF.

## Restoring From A Prior Invoice

PDFs generated by Invoicer include an embedded machine-readable payload. If you upload a previous PDF from the app, Invoicer can use that payload to pre-fill a new invoice.

This is intended to reduce repeated typing. You should still review every field before downloading the new invoice.

## Payment Terms

Payment terms can be entered as ordinary calendar days, such as:

```text
30
```

They can also be entered as Net-style business-day terms, such as:

```text
Net-30
```

Business-day calculations can account for supported public holidays where the deployment environment has the relevant holiday library available.

## Outputs

Invoicer exports:

- A PDF invoice.

The downloaded filename is based on the invoice number and recipient where available.

## Privacy Notes

Invoicer may temporarily retain progress for the active workflow and may embed invoice data inside PDFs generated by the app so future invoices can be pre-filled.

Do not include sensitive information unless you are comfortable with it appearing on the invoice PDF and in the embedded restore payload.

## Important Notes

- Invoicer is a document-generation tool, not accounting software.
- It does not provide tax, legal, or accounting advice.
- You are responsible for checking invoice numbers, dates, totals, payment details, tax handling, and client information before sending an invoice.
